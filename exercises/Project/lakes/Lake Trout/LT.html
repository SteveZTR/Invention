Lake trout

Habitat

Lake trout inhabit the deep, cold waters of the Great Lakes and other large northern lakes. They prefer clear, oxygen-rich environments and are most active in cooler seasons.

Key Features

  • Silvery-gray body with light spots and deeply forked tail
  • Adapted to cold, deep freshwater habitats
  • Top predator feeding on smaller fish and aquatic invertebrates
  • Important to Indigenous cultures and regional fisheries
  • Indicator species for ecosystem health and water quality
