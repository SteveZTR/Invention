Red Snapper

Habitat

Red snappers live in the warm coastal waters of the Gulf of Mexico, often near coral reefs, rocky ledges, and shipwrecks. Juveniles inhabit shallower waters, while adults prefer deeper offshore habitats.

Key Features

  • Bright red body with a pinkish hue and pointed snout
  • Medium to large size, reaching up to 40 inches
  • Predatory diet of small fish, squid, and crustaceans
  • Highly valued for commercial and recreational fishing
  • Conservation-managed due to past overfishing
